MO-G

2000s-2010s

MO-G's work focuses on capturing the Southside of Regent Park before gentrification fully took hold. His photographs offer a nostalgic glimpse into the community before the major redevelopment initiatives began in the early 2000s. These images highlight the vibrant, tight-knit community, showcasing the everyday life of residents in an area that was once a center of multicultural interaction and cultural exchange. Jesse Colin Jackson

2007-2017

Jesse Colin Jackson's Regent Park Transformation Series spans over ten years, documenting the significant changes that the neighborhood underwent due to the gentrification process. Jackson's work captures the human aspect of the redevelopment, focusing on both the physical transformation of the area and the emotional impacts on residents. His photographs highlight the resilience of the community, showcasing the pride and challenges of people living in Regent Park as they navigated a new urban reality. Through intimate portraits and evocative cityscapes, Jackson illustrates the tensions between displacement, redevelopment, and the preservation of community identity.
