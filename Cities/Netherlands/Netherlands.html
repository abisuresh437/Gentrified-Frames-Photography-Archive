Bram Belloni

2010s-2020s

Bram Belloni’s xxx ♥ – Amsterdam Encounters (2021) focuses on capturing candid moments in the streets of Amsterdam, reflecting the everyday life of the city amid rapid gentrification. His work highlights the diversity and transience of urban spaces, documenting how neighborhoods change as gentrification takes hold. Belloni’s photographs provide a nuanced view of how different social groups interact within shifting spaces, emphasizing the growing divide between new, wealthier residents and long-time locals. His work captures the human aspect of gentrification, giving voice to the changing faces of Amsterdam’s streets.

Bos Losekoot

2010s-2020s

In Amsterdam Encounters (2021), Bas Losekoot documents the human side of urban transformation in Amsterdam. His approach focuses on spontaneous moments and street-level encounters that reveal the underlying cultural shifts occurring in the city. Losekoot’s work contrasts the rapidly gentrifying areas with the real people who live in them, capturing the intimacy of urban change. His images shed light on how gentrification impacts social life, offering insight into the subtle but profound transformation of neighborhoods and the experience of residents navigating this change.

Sem Langendijk

2022

Sem Langendijk’s Haven (2022) is a deeply introspective project that examines displacement and gentrification in port cities, with a particular focus on Amsterdam. Combining urban landscapes and portraits, Langendijk reflects on the broader implications of urban development and the personal experience of people living in these transforming spaces. His photographs capture the tension between industrialization, urban renewal, and the cultural heritage that is often at risk of being lost in the process. Haven portrays the emotional and physical changes brought on by gentrification, highlighting the impact of these transformations on communities that once thrived in working-class areas.
