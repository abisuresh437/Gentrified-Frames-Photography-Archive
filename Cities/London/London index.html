Andrew Holligan

1980s - 1990s

Using a 1950s Rolleiflex camera, Holligan documented East London before it underwent significant gentrification. His work captured the everyday lives and architectural details of neighborhoods that were later transformed into trendy, affluent areas. His images evoke a sense of nostalgia and loss for the original character of these spaces, highlighting the social and cultural fabric that was eventually replaced.

Stephen Burridge

Mid 2000s

Burridge's photography focuses on preserving the essence of communities impacted by capitalism's expansion. He captured areas on the brink of transformation, emphasizing the diverse cultures and histories that often get erased during redevelopment. His work celebrates the vibrancy and resilience of communities in the face of rapid urban change.

Zed Nelson

2010s

Through his documentary film The Street (2019), Nelson explored the gentrification of Hoxton Street, a working-class neighborhood in Hackney. His project reveals the displacement of long-time residents, the rise of luxury developments, and the tensions between tradition and modernization. Nelson's work critiques the erasure of community identity and the social inequalities exacerbated by urban renewal.

Lewis Bush

2010-2015

Bush’s Metropole series (2015) used abstract photography to depict London’s aggressive redevelopment. His work focused on the visual and symbolic aspects of gentrification, showing the city as a place increasingly dominated by towering structures and privatized spaces. Bush's imagery reflects a dystopian perspective, questioning who the city is being built for.

Jan Enkelmann

2010s-2020s

Enkelmann’s seven-year project documented the transformation of Brixton, a neighborhood long known for its rich cultural heritage. His photographs capture both the vibrancy of the community and the challenges posed by gentrification, including rising rents and the displacement of local businesses. Enkelmann’s work offers a nuanced look at how residents adapt and resist amidst change.
